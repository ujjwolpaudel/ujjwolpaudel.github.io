**Why are less women breastfeeding in Nepal?**. 2024.<br/>
<small>[ <a href="#/" onclick="visib('monop_retail')">Abstract</a> | Draft coming soon] </small>

<div id="monop_retail" style="display: none; text-align: justify; line-height: 1.2" ><small>

 We examine the declining trend in breastfeeding in Nepal. Using 22 years of nationally representative survey data on Nepalese women, we study the effects of age at first marriage, education level, women participation in the labor force, and availability of breast-milk substitutes on the breastfeeding duration. To this end, we employ a series of instrumental variable strategies which let us to quantify some of the reasons behind this decreasing rate. The analysis generates noble insights into the mechanisms of breastfeeding trend in Nepal.
